Big Lever Institute · Exercise 12

Product-Market Fit Sharpener — articulate and validate your venture before you build

In the next 45 minutes you'll turn a rough venture idea into a pressure-tested Product-Market Fit statement — anchored to a specific customer archetype, grounded in real pains or real gains, and already validated against what AI-powered research can find in the market. By the end you'll have a persistent "PMF Sharpener" Project you can return to every time your idea evolves.

Step 0

Before you start

Have three things ready before you begin:

  1. A rough venture idea. 2–3 sentences is enough. It doesn't have to be good yet — that's what this exercise is for.
  2. ~45 minutes of uninterrupted time. The exercise runs best in one sitting because the Sharpener's interview builds momentum.
  3. A Claude account with Research available. The free tier works for most of this; Research/web-search capability is used in Step 3 to validate the idea.

By the end, you'll be able to complete this sentence about your venture with specificity:

The PMF formulation you'll fill in

My new business [X] creates benefits [A], [B], [C], and eliminates pains [D], [E], [F], for customer [G] and customer [H].

That's the fundamental PMF statement. Exactly what you do. Exactly who you do it for. (Pricing and how you charge for it come later — that's a different week of New Venture Planning.)

Step 1 · Setup

Create your My Venture Project

You're going to do everything for this exercise inside one Claude Project. Articulation, research, interpretation, and the installed coach you'll keep — all of it lives in the same place.

  1. Go to claude.ai.
  2. Click Projects in the sidebar, then New Project.
  3. Name it My Venture.
  4. Description (optional): Where my idea lives, gets sharpened, and gets tested.
  5. Don't set Custom Instructions yet — you'll generate them in Step 6.
  6. Click New Chat inside the Project. Rename this chat to PMF Sharpener.

Check: You should be looking at an empty chat named "PMF Sharpener" inside a Project named "My Venture."

Step 2 · Articulate

Interview with the Sharpener

Paste this prompt into your PMF Sharpener chat and fill in the bracketed field. Go through the interview. Expect 6–10 exchanges before the Sharpener concludes your articulation is ready to validate. Some of what it surfaces may surprise you — stay with it.

Starter prompt — paste into your PMF Sharpener chat
I'm working through BLI's PMF Sharpener exercise. I have a rough idea for a new venture, and I need you to help me sharpen my product-market fit before I build anything.

By the end of this conversation, I should be able to complete this sentence with specificity:

"My new business [X] creates benefits [A], [B], [C], and eliminates pains [D], [E], [F], for customer [G] and customer [H]."

Your job is to interview me until every blank can be filled in concretely:

1. **Benefits (gains) and pains.** Real ones, not a list of features. Not a conceptual abstraction. Benefits and pains can both be present — what we will NOT accept is just features.

2. **Customer archetype(s).** Precise enough that we could google them. Not "small business owners" — "solo HVAC contractors in US metros with 3–15 employees who still do quoting by phone." Up to two archetypes is fine if I really serve two.

3. **Must-have evidence.** What are they doing today to cope with these pains or go without these benefits? What does it cost them — in time, money, revenue, or stress? If they're doing nothing about it, it's probably not must-have.

Rules for the interview:
- If I describe a feature, reflect it back as the underlying benefit or pain and make me restate.
- If my customer description is vague, push me until it's specific enough to find them.
- If I can't articulate what they do today to cope, we haven't found must-have yet.
- Ask one question at a time. Don't let me off the hook.

My rough idea, in 2–3 sentences:
[DESCRIBE YOUR IDEA HERE]

When my articulation is ready, close the interview by doing two things:

(a) Restate my completed PMF formulation back to me in a clean block using the exact sentence structure above.

(b) Generate a deep research prompt I can paste into a fresh chat inside this same Project to validate the idea. The research prompt should ask for evidence that the customer archetype(s) exist and are actively seeking solutions (forums, Reddit threads, reviews, hiring ads, complaints, trade publications), a survey of existing products/services attempting to serve this archetype (including the unsexy ones and the dying ones), market-size proxies, and signals that the pain/gain is must-have (willingness to pay, revenue at stake, cost of workarounds). The research prompt MUST demand raw findings only — no interpretation, no recommendations, no "this seems like a good idea." Output the research prompt as a clean code block I can copy.

Start the interview.

Check: By the end of Step 2 you should have (a) your PMF formulation restated cleanly in the chat, and (b) a research prompt sitting in a code block, ready to copy.

Step 3 · Research

Run the research in a second chat

Copy the research prompt the Sharpener produced at the end of Step 2.

Inside the same My Venture Project, click New Chat. Rename this chat to Research Scratchpad. This is a different workspace for a different job — the Sharpener does articulation and interpretation; the Scratchpad runs the research.

In the new chat:

  1. Paste the research prompt.
  2. Enable Research / web-search mode if your Claude UI offers a toggle for it (look in the chat composer).
  3. Send the prompt. This may take 1–3 minutes — Claude is searching the web.
  4. When the output arrives, review it: does it read like raw findings with sources, or like an essay with opinions?
If the output includes interpretation or recommendations, reply in the Research Scratchpad: "Strip the interpretation. Give me findings only, with sources." Repeat until the output is clean evidence.

When the findings are clean, copy the full output. You'll paste it into the Sharpener chat in Step 4.

Step 4 · Interpret

Back to the Sharpener

Return to your PMF Sharpener chat inside the same Project. Paste this prompt, then paste your research findings where indicated.

Interpretation prompt — paste into your PMF Sharpener chat
Here are the raw research findings from my Research Scratchpad chat. Interpret them honestly against my PMF formulation. I want:

1. Does the evidence support that my customer archetype(s) exist and are actively seeking solutions? Where is that signal strongest? Where is it weakest or missing?

2. Who are the existing solutions in this space? Competitors are VALIDATION — they prove a market exists. List the top 3–5 and describe how each one approaches the problem.

3. If there are NO competitors and NO customer-seeking signals, call that out directly. No results usually means no market, not untapped Blue Ocean. True Blue Ocean is rare enough to be suspicious when it shows up.

4. Is the evidence of must-have-ness (willingness to pay, cost of status quo, current workaround pain) strong, medium, or weak?

5. What are the 3 riskiest assumptions I'm still making that the research couldn't validate — and that I'll need to test with real customers or real experiments?

Raw findings:
[PASTE RAW RESEARCH OUTPUT HERE]

Read the interpretation carefully. This is where a lot of ideas die — or get sharpened into something real. If the interpretation surfaces a reality check that changes your idea, that's the exercise working. Go back to Step 2, tighten the articulation, and rerun.

Step 5 · Synthesize

Write your refined PMF Statement

Still in the PMF Sharpener chat:

Synthesis prompt — paste into your PMF Sharpener chat
Based on the full exercise so far — articulation, research, and interpretation — draft my refined Product-Market Fit Statement using this exact structure:

**My new business [X] creates benefits [A], [B], [C], and eliminates pains [D], [E], [F], for customer [G] and customer [H].**

Fill in every blank with concreteness. No hedging. No vague abstractions. No feature lists.

Then, beneath the statement, include:

- **Evidence of must-have:** what customers do today to cope, what it costs them, willingness-to-pay signals from the research
- **Top 3 competitors and how I differentiate** — or a clear note if I can't differentiate yet and that's the next sharpen
- **3 riskiest assumptions** I still need to validate next (the ones research couldn't answer)

Output as a clean block I can save.

Check: Every blank in the formulation is filled with something specific enough to google. If any element is thin — "small businesses," "various benefits" — push back in the chat: "[X] is still vague — sharpen it." This paragraph is your New Venture Planning Week 1 seed.

Step 6 · Encapsulate

Generate your Custom Instructions

Now turn everything you and the Sharpener just worked through into a reusable coach that lives in this Project permanently.

Encapsulation prompt — paste into your PMF Sharpener chat
Encapsulate this entire conversation into a reusable set of Custom Instructions for my My Venture Project. The instructions should:

- Define the role: PMF Sharpener — a venture coach grounded in Lean Startup and Blue Ocean thinking. Your job is to keep me honest about benefits, pains, archetype, and must-have evidence.

- Always orient toward completing the PMF formulation: "My new business [X] creates benefits [A], [B], [C], and eliminates pains [D], [E], [F], for customer [G] and customer [H]." Every new idea or variant should be tested against this structure.

- Refuse to engage with feature lists. Reflect features back as the underlying benefits or pains and make me restate.

- Force archetype precision — push until the customer is specific enough to google.

- Require must-have evidence — what do they do today to cope? What does it cost them?

- Always recommend pre-build validation via deep research before I commit to building.

- Interpret research results honestly, including calling out "no results usually means no market, not Blue Ocean. True Blue Ocean is rare."

- Carry forward the PMF Statement we just locked as the starting reference point. When I bring a variant, compare it against what we locked today.

Output the Custom Instructions as a clean code block I can paste into the Project's settings.
Step 7 · Install & Invoke

Install the coach and test it

Deliverable — your permanent PMF Sharpener
  1. Paste your Custom Instructions. Open the Project's Custom Instructions (in the My Venture Project's settings). Paste the full CI block from Step 6. Save.
  2. Invoke in a fresh chat. Inside the same My Venture Project, click New Chat. Rename it PMF Test. Paste the invoke prompt below.
Invoke prompt — paste into a new "PMF Test" chat inside the same Project
I want to test a variant of my idea.

Variant: [DESCRIBE A VARIANT — e.g., same customer archetype, different solution approach; OR same benefits/pains, different customer archetype; OR narrower archetype with the same offering]

Sharpen it. Compare against the PMF Statement we locked earlier in this Project.

If the installed Custom Instructions are working, the PMF Test chat will interview you with the same discipline as Step 2 — no onboarding required. It will also reference the PMF Statement you locked earlier, because your Custom Instructions told it to. That persistent, specific coach is what you keep.

Step 8 · ExtendOptional · ~15 min

Turn your 3 riskiest assumptions into a validation plan

The core deliverable is done — you have a working PMF Sharpener and a locked PMF Statement. You can come back to this step anytime. It turns the 3 riskiest assumptions from Step 5 into a week-long experiment plan, without writing any code.

Inside the same My Venture Project, return to your PMF Sharpener chat (it already has all the context) and paste:

Validation-plan prompt — paste into your PMF Sharpener chat
Take the 3 riskiest assumptions from my PMF Statement and turn them into a pre-build validation plan. For each assumption, design one low-cost, fast experiment I can run this week without writing code. Consider:

- 5 customer interviews with a target script
- A landing page test with a specific CTA and a conversion signal
- A manual "wizard of oz" concierge version (I do the service by hand for the first few customers)
- A waitlist test with a tentative pricing tier
- A cold outreach campaign where reply rate is the signal

For each experiment: what I'll do, how long it takes, what signal tells me the assumption holds, what signal tells me it's broken.

Where to go next

Bring your PMF Statement into New Venture Planning Week 1 as your starting seed. Come back to this Project every time your idea shifts — after customer interviews, after a landing page test, after a competitor moves — and rerun the PMF Test chat. The coach will tell you what got sharper and what got murkier.
